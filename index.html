Chore signups! Volunteer/Chore Shifts

Add Yourself to the People Sheet: (link provided after signup and dues)

Our camp provides its members with food and shared facilities. We also provide art and gifts for the community. In order to make that happen, we need everyone in the camp to help out with camp food or with running the cafe.

Each member must commit to at least two chore shifts. This year's shifts are longer but more comprehensive, so cooking and cleaning have been consolidated per meal. If you would like to help and can manage additional shifts beyond the minimum, the camp benefits significantly. Certain shifts like burn night cook are also especially helpful.

Prioritize core shifts over overflow slots. Essential camp functions need coverage first. Consider coordinating with a friend to take a shift with someone you know.

What is Burning Man like?

When & Where: Aug 24-Sep 1, in the desert in north Nevada

It is a city built in an enormous flat desert that is made of a single substance (dust). There are no plants or land features or bugs or anything. All the things there are the things that the people in the city brought, and they bring beautiful strange surplus and share it with you freely.

Googling this question is helpful - the things that people like about it are often pretty personal and diverse.

What makes it special:

  • It is totally apart from Society and is instead some other society, which makes it a good container for thinking about your life
  • Self-reliance and making things with your campmates can be meaningful
  • You can help people have a better Burning Man (like becoming a ranger - a volunteer position where you walk around the city solving people's problems and you have a radio)
  • Some people like to get high or do weird sex stuff - those activities are abundantly available
  • You can ride your bike and look at pretty art all week and not run out
  • If you want, you get to help build big things from metal, or bring your own art or whatever

Practical considerations:

  • It's hot in the day and cold at night and always dusty
  • Survival isn't that hard - it's just camping
  • The camp provides some infrastructure (shade, meals, being in a good place in the city, etc)
  • Important to know going in that Burning Man does, like, suck
